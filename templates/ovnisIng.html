What is your function

for

An omnidirectional antenna is characterized by radiating power into space uniformly and in all directions, with the exception of the isotropic omnidirectional antenna, which acts in three dimensions. This antenna is a theoretical construction that is used as a reference to calculate the gain of an antenna. Another of its uses is to calculate the effective power that radiates in radio frequency systems, usually emitters and telecommunications receivers. In practice, omnidirectional antenna is understood to be those antennas that provide uniform radiation in only one reference plane. Conventionally, the plane of uniform radiation is the plane parallel to the surface of the Earth.
